Creamy Mushroom Risotto

Luxurious comfort in every bite

This velvety mushroom risotto is the ultimate comfort food. Rich, creamy, and packed with earthy mushroom flavors, it's perfect for a cozy dinner at home or impressing guests at your next gathering.

Prep Time 15 mins
Cook Time 30 mins
Servings 4 people
Difficulty Medium

Ingredients

Main Ingredients

  • 1½ cups Arborio rice
  • 300g mixed mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 5 cups vegetable stock, warm
  • ½ cup dry white wine
  • ½ cup Parmesan cheese, grated
  • 3 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Optional Garnish

  • Fresh parsley, chopped
  • Extra Parmesan shavings
  • Truffle oil (for luxury)

Instructions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pan and sauté mushrooms until golden. Set aside.
  2. In the same pan, add 1 tablespoon butter and sauté onion until translucent. Add garlic and cook for 1 minute.
  3. Add Arborio rice and toast for 2 minutes, stirring constantly until edges become translucent.
  4. Pour in white wine and stir until absorbed.
  5. Add warm stock one ladle at a time, stirring frequently. Wait until each addition is absorbed before adding more.
  6. After about 20 minutes, when rice is creamy and al dente, stir in mushrooms, remaining butter, and Parmesan.
  7. Season with salt and pepper. Serve immediately with garnish of your choice.

Pro Tips

  • Keep the stock warm throughout cooking for best results
  • Stir frequently but not constantly - every 30 seconds is perfect
  • For extra depth, add dried porcini mushrooms soaked in warm water
  • Substitute wine with extra stock if preferred

Honey Garlic Salmon

Quick and healthy weeknight winner

Sweet, savory, and perfectly flaky - this honey garlic salmon comes together in under 20 minutes. It's a nutritious, restaurant-quality meal that's surprisingly simple to make at home.

Prep Time 5 mins
Cook Time 12 mins
Servings 4 people
Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (about 150g each)
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 3 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 1 teaspoon ginger, grated
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Sesame seeds for garnish
  • Green onions, sliced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Pat salmon fillets dry and season with salt and pepper.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together honey, soy sauce, garlic, lemon juice, and ginger.
  3. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Place salmon skin-side up and sear for 4 minutes until golden.
  5. Flip salmon and pour honey garlic sauce over the fillets.
  6. Cook for another 4-5 minutes, spooning sauce over salmon frequently, until cooked through.
  7. Garnish with sesame seeds and green onions. Serve hot with rice or vegetables.

Pro Tips

  • Don't overcook - salmon should be slightly pink in the center
  • For crispy skin, start cooking skin-side down and don't move it
  • Double the sauce recipe if you like it extra saucy
  • Works beautifully with trout or arctic char too

Classic Tiramisu

Italian indulgence perfected

Layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and mascarpone cream create this iconic Italian dessert. No-bake and make-ahead friendly, it's the perfect ending to any dinner party or a luxurious weekend treat.

Prep Time 30 mins
Chill Time 4 hours
Servings 8 people
Difficulty Easy

Ingredients

Mascarpone Cream

  • 500g mascarpone cheese
  • 4 large eggs, separated
  • ¾ cup granulated sugar
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

Assembly

  • 300g ladyfinger biscuits
  • 1½ cups strong espresso, cooled
  • 3 tablespoons coffee liqueur (optional)
  • Cocoa powder for dusting
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Instructions

  1. Beat egg yolks with sugar until pale and thick (about 3 minutes). Add vanilla.
  2. Gently fold in mascarpone until smooth and creamy.
  3. In a separate bowl, whip egg whites to stiff peaks.
  4. Carefully fold egg whites into mascarpone mixture in three additions.
  5. Mix espresso with coffee liqueur in a shallow dish.
  6. Quickly dip ladyfingers in coffee (1 second each side) and arrange in a single layer in a dish.
  7. Spread half the mascarpone cream over ladyfingers. Repeat layers.
  8. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours (overnight is best).
  9. Before serving, dust generously with cocoa powder and add chocolate shavings.

Pro Tips

  • Don't over-soak ladyfingers or they'll become mushy
  • Use room temperature mascarpone for easier mixing
  • For alcohol-free version, skip the liqueur and use vanilla in coffee
  • Make it 24 hours ahead for best flavor development
